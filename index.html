Scholarship: Research Training Program

I am a PhD researcher at the University of Adelaide, specializing in the impact of climate change on building energy performance and the carbon footprint of sustainable construction materials, particularly cross-laminated timber buildings.

My research focuses on developing quantitative methodologies to assess how climate change affects thermal building performance and energy conservation measures. I combine life cycle assessment techniques with building energy simulation to understand the long-term environmental impacts of different construction approaches.

With a strong background in architecture and sustainable design, I have published extensively in top-tier journals and am actively involved in international research collaborations, including the IEA EBC Annex 89 project on implementing net-zero whole life carbon buildings.
